Linux was created in 1991 by Linus Torvalds as a hobby project to create a free operating system kernel. Inspired by MINIX, he released it under the GNU General Public License, and the rest is history. Linux has grown into a powerful family of open-source OSes that power everything from smartphones to supercomputers!

üêß What is Linux?

Linux is a free, open-source operating system kernel originally created by Linus Torvalds in 1991. It powers everything from smartphones and servers to supercomputers and embedded systems, offering unparalleled flexibility and control.

üìö A Brief History

1991

Linux kernel announced by Linus Torvalds as a hobby project.

1993

First major distributions: Slackware, Debian, and Red Hat emerge.

2007

Android brings Linux to billions of mobile devices worldwide.

Today

Linux dominates servers, mobile, cloud, and embedded systems.
